# Launch-week schedule — block by block

**Target launch day:** Tuesday of launch week. Never Monday (HN flood), never Friday (HN dead).
**Assumptions:** all pre-launch prep (Phase 0) is done. Domain is live. Stripe + Supabase + Resend + GitHub are all wired.

## Monday — final prep + prime the pump

**09:00** — Run your own `/board:weekly`. Lock 3 priorities. Priority 1 is "launch ships at 07:00 Pacific tomorrow."
**10:00** — Record the demo video (Scene 1–7 from demo-video-script.md). First take; don't overthink. Export 3 versions.
**13:00** — Final smoke test of the purchase flow. Real Stripe test card. Real email. Real GitHub username. Real invite arrives.
**15:00** — DM 10 founder friends: "Shipping tomorrow 07:00 Pacific — here's a sneak link, free to browse. Would mean a lot if you could share after launch." Don't ask for buys. Ask for eyes.
**18:00** — Post a "Launching CxOPack tomorrow 07:00 Pacific" tease on X + LinkedIn. One sentence. No link.

## Tuesday — launch day

**06:45** — At desk. Coffee. Notifications off except the tools.
**07:00** — Post Show HN. Paste the prepared first-comment FAQ.
**07:15** — Post the X thread. Quote-tweet the Show HN post in tweet 7.
**08:00 EU** — Post the LinkedIn launch post with the 90-second demo clip.
**09:00 EU** — Send the waitlist email to the first 30 addresses.
**09:30** — Check in on HN, X, LinkedIn. Reply to everything.
**10:00 Pacific** — Indie Hackers post goes live.
**10:00–16:00 Pacific** — Sit at the desk. Reply to every comment, every DM, every tweet. This is the job today.
**16:00 Pacific** — First count of the day. Screenshot: visitors, Launch-100 subscribers, demo runs, revenue. Save for tomorrow's recap.
**19:00** — Shut off, eat, sleep. Tomorrow is the real work.

## Wednesday — "Day 1 by the numbers"

**08:00** — Post the "Launch day results — 24h" update on X and LinkedIn. Real numbers, no spin.
**10:00** — DM every Launch-100 customer personally: "Thank you. Reply with one sentence about what you'll try first." Half will reply. That's your feedback pipeline for the week.
**14:00** — Respond to any HN comments from overnight (European time zones).
**16:00** — Start drafting the "What I got wrong" post for Thursday.

## Thursday — mid-week follow-up

**09:00** — Post "What I got wrong (launch day retro)" mini-thread on X. Honest self-critique.
**11:00** — Email the first 10 Launch-100 customers directly: "What's the first workflow you ran? What happened?" Personal replies only — no template.
**14:00** — Sales call: jump on a 15-min call with anyone who replied to the day-0 email with a real use case. Learn more about them than about the product.
**16:00** — Feedback triage: read all Launch-100 replies so far. Note the top 3 patterns.

## Friday — retro + fix cycle

**09:00** — Run `/ceo:journal` on the launch week. Three wins, one failure, one lesson, one Monday recalibration. Post it publicly.
**11:00** — Ship the #1 feedback issue from the week. Small fixes only — no refactors. Push, deploy.
**15:00** — Plan next week's content. Pick the first customer story you can publish. Ask for permission.
**17:00** — Shut off for the weekend.

## Saturday/Sunday — rest, actively

- No coding. No email. No Twitter.
- One afternoon walk. Bring a notebook.
- Write down what the product should be 90 days from now. Not tomorrow. 90 days.

## Monday (launch week + 1)

**09:00** — Run `/board:weekly`. Priorities for this week are *not* "more launch." They're "grow retention for the 100 people who bought."

## Weekly rhythm from here

- Monday 09:00 — `/board:weekly` or `/ceo:weekly`
- Daily 10:00 (5 min) — post one thing (LinkedIn / X). Topic came from last week's `linkedin-batch` output.
- Tuesday & Thursday afternoons — direct customer calls. At least 1/week for the first 3 months.
- Friday 17:00 — `/ceo:journal` retro.
- First Monday of each month — `/cfo:saas-metrics` + `/cfo:investor-update` — even if no investors. The discipline matters.
- Last Thursday of each month — `/sales:icp` refresh with the latest customer signal.

## What to NOT do in launch week

- New features
- Big refactors
- Twitter arguments
- Comparisons to other products (unless a customer initiates)
- Apologizing for bugs before anyone reports them
- Checking analytics more than 3x per day
