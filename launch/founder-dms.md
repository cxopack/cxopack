# Pre-launch founder DMs

Ten warm DMs sent 48–72h before public launch. Goal: **5 first paying customers and 5 honest "not for me / here's why"** — both are useful signal.

Not a mass send. Personalise the first sentence and the "why you" line for every recipient. If you can't say something specific about their work, drop them.

---

## Who to message

Pick from people who already know you, or whose work you've genuinely engaged with in the last 60 days. Rough bucket:

1. 3–4 indie founders in your network (Paris/Twitter/Indie Hackers).
2. 2–3 solo technical founders who've shipped an MVP recently.
3. 2–3 non-technical founders who lean on AI heavily.
4. 1 friendly operator/advisor who'll be blunt.

Avoid: strangers, people you haven't interacted with in months, anyone who would feel "sold to."

## Channel

X DM > LinkedIn DM > email. Use the channel where you've already had conversation with them.

---

## The message

**Subject (email only):** Quick one before I launch

**Body:**

> Hey {{firstName}} — {{one sentence about something specific they shipped or said recently, e.g. "saw your thread on the pivot to B2B, loved the part about pricing."}}.
>
> I'm launching CxOPack on {{date}} — think of it as a pack of AI "C-suite" kits for solo founders: CEO / CTO / CFO / Sales / CMO workflows you run inside Claude Code, ChatGPT, or Cursor. The Board kit lets a Chief of Staff subagent ("Donna") route a single request to whichever exec it needs.
>
> Two reasons I'm pinging you specifically:
>
> 1. {{why you — e.g. "you've been vocal about solo founders needing leverage, not more dashboards"}}.
> 2. I want brutal feedback before I go broad. Working or not, I'd rather hear it from you than from Show HN.
>
> If you want in, there's a Launch-100 slot at €99/yr forever (normal price €149/yr). Here's the link: {{launchLink}}. If it's not for you, a "here's what would have made me bite" reply is genuinely as useful to me as a sale.
>
> Either way — thanks for reading. No pressure, no follow-up if you pass.

---

## Variants by archetype

**Technical solo founder** — replace the 1/2 block with:

> 1. You've been building solo and I think the CTO + CFO kits would save you the weekends you currently burn on runway math and arch decisions.
> 2. I want brutal feedback before I go broad.

**Non-technical founder** — replace with:

> 1. You've said before that you wanted a way to "pressure-test decisions without booking a call." The Board kit is literally that — you ask Donna, he pulls in CEO + CFO + whoever else, you get a second opinion in 2 minutes.
> 2. I'd rather hear it from you than from strangers on HN.

**Operator / advisor (no sale expected)** — replace the offer line with:

> I don't expect you to buy — I'd love 10 minutes of your read on the positioning and whether the Board angle actually lands. Happy to send a loom instead of a call if easier.

---

## What to do with the replies

- **Buy:** send them the Launch-100 link, then a thank-you + a direct line to you for the first 30 days. They are your first case study.
- **"Not for me because X":** record X in `founder-log/feedback.md`. If the same X comes up twice, it's a real gap — flag it for post-launch week 1.
- **Silence:** do not follow up. The whole pitch was "no pressure, no follow-up."

## Tone rules

- No "reaching out," no "exciting news," no "would love to jump on a quick call."
- Make it sound like one founder writing to another at 11pm, not a marketing sequence.
- If the DM is longer than ~180 words, cut it.
- Never send the same DM twice — not even to two different people. Every one needs a personalised first line, or it's not worth sending.
