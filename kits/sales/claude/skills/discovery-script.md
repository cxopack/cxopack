# Discovery Script — 70% listen, 30% frame

## Principles

- **Discovery is NOT pitching.** If you're pitching features, the call failed.
- **Find the pain, the stakes, the buying process — in that order.**
- **Silence is a tool.** After a question, count to 3. The best answers come after the silence, not the first response.
- **Price anchor, don't price negotiate.** Reveal a range only after pain and stakes are established.

## The 45-min structure

### 0-5: Frame the call
- "Here's what I'd love to cover in the next 45 minutes. Does that work?"
- Explicit out: "If at any point this isn't useful, stop me."
- **Do NOT pitch here.** Just frame the conversation.

### 5-20: Pain — what's happening today
- "Walk me through how you [current workflow]. What does that look like day-to-day?"
- "Where does that start to feel painful?"
- "How often does that come up?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What didn't work about those?"

Listen for:
- Quantifiable pain (time lost, money lost, customer churn, missed deals)
- Emotional pain (someone got yelled at, someone left the company over this)

### 20-30: Stakes — what happens if you do nothing
- "If this stays as-is 6 months from now, what breaks?"
- "Who's watching this problem at your company?"
- "What's the cost of not solving it?"

Listen for: **who is pushing for a solution and why now**. No urgency = no deal.

### 30-38: Buying process
- "If you decided to solve this, who else would be involved in the decision?"
- "What's your typical process for evaluating a tool like this?"
- "What budget does this come from? Is it already allocated?"
- "What's your timeline?"

### 38-43: Frame the fit (not the pitch)
- "Based on what you've shared, here are three ways we could help: [A, B, C]. Which of those matters most?"
- Let them rank. That's your proposal outline.

### 43-45: Next step
- "If this sounds like a fit, the next step would be [specific action] by [specific date]. Does that work?"

## Output

Produce a printable one-pager:
```md
# Discovery call — [Prospect] — [Date]

## Research
- Company: …
- Person: …
- Likely pain hypothesis: …
- Known alternatives: …

## Opening frame
"…"

## Questions (in order)
1. …
2. …
…

## Silence reminders
- [ ] Count to 3 after each question
- [ ] Never fill dead air with product pitch

## Listening targets
- [ ] Quantified pain
- [ ] Buying process (who, when, budget)
- [ ] Urgency driver (the "why now")

## Next step options
- If qualified: …
- If not: …
```

## Anti-patterns

- Opening with a demo
- Talking >40% of the time
- Asking about budget in the first 10 minutes
- Ending without a specific next step and date
