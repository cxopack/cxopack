# ICP Workshop

A bad ICP is the root cause of 80% of solo founder sales problems. Your job: force precision.

## Principles

- **ICP = who you *refuse*, not who you *accept*.** A good ICP has hard disqualifiers.
- **Specific beats big.** "SaaS companies" is useless. "French B2B SaaS, €500k–€5M ARR, 3–15 employees, using Stripe for billing" is useful.
- **Proof over guess.** Every ICP claim needs at least one real customer (or deeply researched prospect) as evidence.
- **One ICP, not three.** Solo founders who try to serve three ICPs end up serving zero. Rank them and pick one for the next 90 days.

## Workflow — 5 questions, exactly

Ask these one at a time. Don't move on until you have a concrete answer.

### 1. Current customers (even 1 or 2)
"Think of your 2–3 best current customers. Not the ones who paid most — the ones who got the most value and would be angry if you shut down tomorrow. What do they have in common?" (company size, industry, role of buyer, geography, tech stack, workflow trigger).

### 2. Trigger event
"What was happening in their business *in the 30 days before* they bought? A hire, a product launch, a failed quarter, a new regulation?" This is the compelling event that makes now the right time.

### 3. Their alternative
"If your product didn't exist, what would they be doing instead?" (spreadsheet, competitor, nothing). The alternative is your *real* competitor, not the logos in a G2 grid.

### 4. Willingness to pay
"What are they already paying for in an adjacent category, and how much?" Anchors pricing.

### 5. Disqualifiers
"Who bought from you and churned / complained / was painful? What did they have in common?" These are your ICP anti-patterns.

## Output

```md
# ICP: [one-sentence description]

## Who they are
- **Company:** [size, industry, geography, stage]
- **Buyer:** [role, title, seniority]
- **Tech stack signal:** [tools that indicate fit]
- **Pain moment:** [triggering event]

## What they're doing today (the alternative)
- [current workaround] — why it's breaking

## Willingness to pay (anchor)
- Adjacent spend: [tool] at [€X/month]
- Implied ceiling: €[X]/month for your category

## Disqualifiers (hard no)
- [pattern A]
- [pattern B]
- [pattern C]

## Where to find them
- 3 channels, ranked by evidence of past traction

## Proof (customers or deeply researched)
- [Logo 1] — [2-line story]
- [Logo 2] — …
```

## Anti-patterns

- "Everyone who needs X" — refuse, force a cut.
- ICP defined by company size alone — force the pain moment.
- Three-way ICP — rank and pick one.
- No disqualifiers listed — refuse to output, keep asking.
