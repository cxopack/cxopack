# LinkedIn Batch (7 posts)

## Principles

- **7 posts, 7 formats.** Same topic from 7 angles beats 7 variations of the same angle.
- **Hook > everything.** 80% of the work is the first line. If it doesn't stop the scroll, the rest doesn't matter.
- **One POV per post.** Not "here are 7 things." Say one thing, hard.
- **Specific > inspirational.** "We grew MRR 22%" ✅. "Consistency is key" ❌.

## The 7 formats (one of each per batch)

1. **Hot take** — a pointed opinion that at least 30% of the audience will disagree with.
2. **Mini case study** — specific numbers, before/after, what happened, what you learned.
3. **How-to list** — 5–7 tactical steps, each short enough to scan.
4. **Anti-pattern / mistake** — "I thought X. I was wrong. Here's why."
5. **Behind-the-scenes** — a specific decision, why you made it, what the tradeoff was.
6. **Data point** — a number + its interpretation.
7. **Question post** — genuine curiosity, not rage-bait. The best ones open a conversation you actually want.

## Hook patterns that work

- "Here's what nobody tells you about [specific thing]:"
- "[Specific number]. That's the result of [action]."
- "I stopped doing [common thing]. My [metric] went up [amount]."
- "The fastest way to [outcome] isn't [common advice]. It's [counterintuitive thing]."
- "[Counterintuitive claim]. Let me show you."

## Format rules

- First 3 lines before the "see more" cut-off are the entire job of the hook.
- Short paragraphs (1–2 sentences max). White space = reader's attention.
- No hashtag spam. 0–3 max, specific.
- One CTA question at the end, genuine.
- 800–1200 characters total. Longer doesn't lift engagement.

## Workflow

### 1. Find the topic
Ask what the user shipped / learned / observed this week. If multiple things, pick the one most likely to generate conversation (not the one most likely to get likes).

### 2. Brainstorm angles
Draft 10 angles across the 7 formats. Pick the 7 best.

### 3. Write hooks first
7 hooks, side by side. Read them all together. Cut any that sound same-y.

### 4. Fill in bodies
Now the rest. Bodies can be short — the hook did the work.

### 5. Output
Produce all 7 posts ready to paste, with scheduled day (Mon–Sun).

```md
## Batch: [topic] — week of [date]

### Mon — Hot take
**Hook:** …
[body]

### Tue — Case study
…

(…all 7…)
```

## Anti-patterns

- "Entrepreneurship is a journey" — delete.
- Stacking emojis in hooks.
- Posting the same photo with different text.
- Begging for likes ("if this resonated, like & share").
- Fake humility ("I'm not an expert but…"). Either have the POV or don't post.
