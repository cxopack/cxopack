# Launch Comms

Most solo founders launch once on one channel and call it done. The signal gets lost. The skill produces a **multi-format, multi-angle launch kit** so the same launch lands 5 times in 5 different reader moods.

## The 5 formats, 5 angles

Each format targets a different reader context. Same launch, different frame:

1. **Twitter/X thread** — counter-intuitive or personal angle. 7–10 tweets. Thread structure: hook → tension → reveal → proof → CTA.
2. **LinkedIn post** — professional/outcome angle. Story-driven, 800–1200 chars. One specific number, one reframe, one soft CTA.
3. **Newsletter** — to existing subscribers. Reflective or insider angle. 250–400 words. Assumes reader already cares.
4. **PR pitch / cold outbound to journalist** — news-hook angle. 1 paragraph + 1 bullet list of what's interesting. Specific reason why they'd care.
5. **Product Hunt / Hacker News** — direct product angle. First comment from founder handles objections upfront.

## Writing principles

- **One launch = one headline thought.** All 5 formats point at the same insight; the framing varies.
- **Hooks do 80% of the work.** Every format's first line must pass the 3-second test.
- **No "excited to announce."** Replace with the actual news.
- **Same proof across formats.** The number or customer quote is identical; only the context changes.

## The 5 format templates

### Twitter/X thread (7-10 tweets)
```md
1/ <Hook — counter-intuitive claim or personal reveal. Max 200 chars.>

2/ <Tension — the problem this launch solves. Make it real and specific.>

3/ <The reframe — what you realized or built. Still no pitch.>

4-6/ <Proof — 3 tweets of the real evidence: numbers, screenshots, customer wins.>

7/ <The product, named for the first time. 1 sentence. Link or how to get it.>

8/ <"Built this because <personal reason>. Here's what's next: <roadmap in 1 line>.">

9/ <"Questions? Reply below. I'll answer all of them."> (optional)

10/ <Retweet cue: "If this resonated, the first tweet again is here: <link>"> (optional)
```

### LinkedIn post (800-1200 chars)
```md
<Hook — a specific moment or decision, sounds personal>

<1-paragraph story: what you were doing, what went wrong, what you learned>

<The reframe: what you built as a result>

<Proof point: one number, one logo, or one specific outcome>

<One soft CTA: "Curious? Link in comments." or "DM me 'CxO' and I'll send.">

<1-2 relevant hashtags — no spam>
```

### Newsletter (250-400 words)
```md
Subject: <specific, not "exciting launch">
Preview: <9-12 words, gives the punchline>

Hey —

<Hook — one or two sentences, lands in the first visible pane of the email>

<What's new in 1 paragraph: the launch, not a feature list>

<Why it matters to them: specific to subscriber context>

<Proof or preview: numbers, screenshot link, customer quote>

<Clear CTA: one link, one action, one sentence>

<Sign-off — personal tone>

Talk soon,
<Name>

P.S. <The thing you actually wanted to say but couldn't fit above — often the best-performing line>
```

### PR pitch (journalist cold email)
```md
Subject: <news hook + specific angle, not "press release">

Hi <Journalist name>,

<1-sentence personalization — a specific piece they wrote recently>

<1-paragraph news: what launched, what's novel, why it matters to the reader base they serve>

A few specifics that might make this interesting to cover:
- <Specific stat or first that others don't have>
- <Customer name or number you can share>
- <Why now — the timing hook>

Happy to share more, on or off the record. 15-min call this week?

<Name>
<Phone, email, site>
```

### Product Hunt / Show HN first comment
```md
<Greeting, 1-2 sentences about who you are and why you built this>

What it does: <plain sentence, no marketing speak>

Why we built it: <the original problem, in your own words>

What's different: <1-2 specific things, honest about what it is and isn't>

Today's ask: <specific feedback you want — "brutal feedback on pricing" / "tell me if the positioning is clear">

Thanks for the time — I'll answer every comment on this thread today.
```

## Workflow

1. Ask for: what's launching (1 sentence), the specific proof point (number or customer), the core insight or story behind it.
2. Generate all 5 formats. Each must share the same proof and same core insight — different frames only.
3. For the Twitter thread and LinkedIn, write the hooks first across formats and compare. If they sound similar, rewrite. Variety across formats is the point.
4. For the newsletter, write the subject + preview + first paragraph first. Those are 80% of the open rate.
5. For PR, require the journalist's name + a specific piece they wrote — no generic pitches.
6. Save to `content/launches/YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>/` with one file per format.

## Cross-channel timing

For a high-stakes launch (new product, raise, big customer):
- T-0 (Tuesday 10am Pacific): Product Hunt / HN post goes live.
- T+15min: Twitter thread.
- T+1h: LinkedIn post.
- T+2h: Newsletter sends to subscribers.
- T+1d: PR pitches go out.

Don't do all 5 at once on a random Sunday. Timing matters.

## Anti-patterns

- **Launch day = announcement day only.** The launch should have been discussed for 2 weeks prior (build-in-public) so the launch has momentum before the "official" day.
- **Cross-posting the same text everywhere.** Each channel has a reader mood; same text means failing 4 of 5 moods.
- **"We are thrilled to announce"** — the text equivalent of a corporate press release. Delete.
- **Self-congratulatory tone without customer benefit.** Every launch is about the customer, not you.
- **Too many CTAs.** One action per post. Don't list 4 places to learn more.
- **No follow-up.** The launch isn't day 1 — it's days 1-7. Plan the follow-up content on day 2, 4, 7.

## Follow-up content (week of)

After the launch, write:
- **Day 2:** "Launch day numbers" — transparency post. Real traffic, real signups, real revenue.
- **Day 4:** "What I got wrong" — the thing the launch post glossed over.
- **Day 7:** "First customer story" — specific use case, with permission.

These three follow-ups extend the launch attention window from 1 day to 1 week.
